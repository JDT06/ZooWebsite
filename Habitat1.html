Rainforest Habitat

Photo from https://kids.nationalgeographic.com/nature/habitats/article/rain-forest

The rainforest habitat is home to many animals big and small. Here we will show you 2 of the animals we have from a rainforest. The rainforest is warm, full of lots of tally trees and leafy plants. The rainforest also gets lots of rain each year hence the name.

Capybara

Photo from https://www.rainforest-alliance.org/everyday-actions/11-amazing-rainforest-animals/

The Capybara is the biggest rodent in the world. It can weigh more than 100 pounds and stand two feet tall. The Capybara jumps into water to hide from predators and can hold its breath for up to five minutes. The Capybara is a very peaceful animal and likes to be with other animals that don't hurt it. The Capybara is a herbivore

Black Howler Monkey

Photo from https://www.rainforest-alliance.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/black-howler-monkey-fullsize.jpg.webp

The Black Howler Monkey earned its name from the loud howl it uses to mark territory. Their howl sounds like a strong wind blowing through a tunnel. Their howl can be heard up to two miles away. They live high in tall rain forest trees, and they also live in groups of 4 to 19.
