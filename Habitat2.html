Desert Habitat

https://cdn.britannica.com/39/142139-050-7EFA4015/Simpson-Desert-Australia.jpg?w=300

A desert is considered a place that gets less than 25 centimeters of rain per year. Deserts cover more than one fifth of the Earth's land area and are found on every continent. Lots of animals and plants have adapted to the harsh environment in the desert. Deserts can be hot and cold, the largest hot desert is the Sahara desert in Africa. The Gobi desert in Asia is always cold.

Mojave Desert Tortoise

Photo from https://www.activewild.com/desert-animals/

Until 2011 the Mojave desert tortoise was thought to be the same species as the Sonoran desert tortoise. The tortoise can be found in the north and west of the Colorado River in California, Nevada, Utah, and Arizona. This tortoise spends up to 95% of its time in burrows or in hidden rock shelters.

Deathstalker Scorpion

Photo from https://www.activewild.com/desert-animals/

Has one of the most powerful venom of all scorpions. Its sting is very painful and might be fatal. The scorpion is about 6cm in length and is a variety of colors and markings to blend in. This scorpion might sound all bad but its venom can be used to locate tumors in the body.
