Swamp Habitat

Photo from https://gorp.com/visiting-the-okefenokee-swamp/

Swamps are a type of wetland, and they can be found throughout the world. They are often found with association with rivers. Trees are the most common vegetation in swamps. Lots of different animals live in swamps from tree frogs to crocodiles.

Roseate Spoonbill

Photo from https://www.animalspot.net/roseate-spoonbill.html

Male spoonbills are much larger than females and their length is about between 28 and 34 inches. Their wingspan can stretch to about 47 to 50 inches long. They're light and only weigh about 1.2 to 1.8 kg. The adult birds have a dark pink tinge on their bodies. Young birds have mostly white and some pink tints in some places. Hence, the name Roseate Spoonbills have a wide bill that resembles a spoon.

Nile Crocodile

Photo from https://www.animalspot.net/nile-crocodile.html

The Nile crocodile has extremely powerful jaws and has a valve at the back of its throat to stop water from coming in. Its tail is long, strong, and has spikes going down it like its back. It also has four long and webbed feet. The adult Nile crocodile is grayish on its back and yellowish underneath. Some older Nile crocodiles can be about 18ft long. The males can be between 11ft to 16ft while the females can be between 8ft to 13ft in length. The average healthy adult Nile crocodile can weigh about 225kg.
