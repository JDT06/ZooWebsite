Sea Otter

Photo from https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_otter

Sea otters are usually found in kelp beds floating on its back. Sea otters break the shells on their food by hitting it with a stone. Sea otters are the largest otters reaching 100-160cm long and weighing 16-40kg when fully grown. Sea otters mostly eat sea urchins, crabs, and shellfish. When sea otters are sleeping they hold on to each other and kelp to not float away.

Dolphin

Photo from https://www.britannica.com/animal/dolphin-mammal

Dolphins are one of the mammals in water that have to go to the surface to breathe. Dolphins are noted by many as playful and intelligent. Thanks to their intelligence and playful nature many dolphins are used in zoos and aquariums to perform tricks. Most dolphins are small, measuring less than 10 feet in length. Dolphins have beak like snouts, needle like teeth, and smooth long bodies.
