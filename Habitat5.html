Mountain Habitat

Photo from https://www.britannica.com/science/mountain-landform

Mountains are rocky areas prominently above its surroundings. They can have caves and little holes and are home to animals big and small. Most of the time mountains are in groups and called mountain ranges. Mountain belts occur when more than one mountain range are together forming a line.

Andean Condor

Photo from https://www.activewild.com/mountain-animals/

The Andean condor is one of the biggest birds in the world with a wingspan around 9ft. Only four other birds have a larger wingspan than the Andean condor. The Andean condor is also one of the heaviest flying birds weighing in around 25lb. This bird feeds mainly on carrion like other vultures. This bird only flaps its wings 1% of the time while its flying and soars the other time. The Andean condor lives in the Andes mountain range in South America.

Chinchilla

Photo from https://www.activewild.com/mountain-animals/

Chinchillas are part of the rodent family and live in the Andes mountain range. Their fur is the densest of any land mammal; this adaptation was due to them living in the mountains. The two species of chinchillas are the short-tailed and long-tailed chinchillas. The short-tailed species is the longest of the two with a body length reaching about 19 in. and has a shorter tail and smaller ears than the long-tailed. Both specie of chinchilla are endangered in the wild.
